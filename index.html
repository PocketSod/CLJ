Our Model

A cooperative owned by language workers

ILJC is a worker-owned cooperative — our interpreters, translators, and language justice professionals are the owners. Every decision, every policy, every service rate is shaped by the people doing the work.

We believe language access is a human right. Rooted in Indianapolis, we serve Marion County's richly multilingual communities with the depth and respect they deserve — while building sustainable livelihoods for language workers.

"Language justice means more than translation. It means the right to be fully understood — and to fully understand."

01

Democratic Ownership

Workers own and govern the cooperative. One worker, one vote — every voice counts in how we run.

02

Living Wages

We are committed to sustainable, fair compensation for all language workers — no race to the bottom.

03

Community Accountability

We answer to the communities we serve, not shareholders. Local, transparent, mission-driven.

04

Language as Liberation

Access to language is a matter of dignity, equity, and justice. That is why we do this work.

Impact

Our work moves institutions toward equity

When language barriers fall, communities thrive. Our members have delivered thousands of hours of services that transform how Indianapolis institutions serve multilingual residents.

From hospital waiting rooms to school intake offices, ILJC members ensure that language is never a barrier to access, dignity, or safety.
